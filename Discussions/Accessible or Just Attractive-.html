Your Turn: Evaluate Design Choices

You've just seen what separates accessible design from inaccessible design. The good LMS wasn't just prettier, it was functionally better for ALL users because it followed WCAG principles and UDL thinking.

Now comes the hard question: How do you balance aesthetics with accessibility in your own course?

Some educators worry that accessible design means boring design. That's a myth. The best course design is both beautiful AND functional; it just requires intentional choices.

Let's explore this tension together.

As an educator designing courses in an LMS, you often make choices about layout, visuals, and navigation. Think about two design approaches:

  1. A page that is visually polished but may have accessibility barriers.
  2. A page that prioritizes accessibility and usability for all learners.

Questions to Consider:

  • Which design better supports diverse learners, including students with disabilities or neurodiverse learners?
  • How might certain visual or stylistic choices sometimes conflict with usability or accessibility in an LMS?
  • How could you balance aesthetics with accessibility when creating your own course content?

Instructions:

  • Post your reflections in the “Accessible or Just Attractive?” discussion board.
  • Include specific examples from course pages you’ve seen, created, or can imagine in your LMS.
  • Respond to at least one classmate’s post, adding insight, questions, or alternative strategies.

This discussion helps you critically evaluate design choices in your LMS, encouraging solutions that are both attractive and inclusive for all learners.
