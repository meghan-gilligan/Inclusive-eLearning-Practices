Welcome to Inclusive eLearning Practices!

Course Description

Inclusive eLearning Practices is a learner-centred course designed to equip educators and designers with practical strategies for creating accessible digital learning environments.

Drawing on principles of Universal Design for Learning (UDL), this course demonstrates how a centralized Learning Management System (LMS), such as Canvas, can be intentionally used to reduce the cognitive overload that disproportionately affects neurodivergent students.

Learners will explore methods for building consistent navigation, designing accessible content, and applying UDL guidelines to enhance engagement and equity across higher education and workplace learning contexts. This short course models the exact inclusive practices it teaches, serving as a practical proof of concept for accessible digital design.

Course Learning Outcomes

On the successful completion of the course, students will be able to:

LO1. Define neurodiversity and identify common challenges that neurodivergent students experience in conventional, fragmented online learning environments.

LO2. Explain the critical importance of inclusive eLearning and its direct impact on neurodivergent student engagement, retention, and overall academic success.

LO3. Apply the core principles of Universal Design for Learning (UDL) to create course content that offers multiple means of engagement, representation, and action/expression.

LO4. Implement practical design strategies that reduce cognitive load and remove accessibility barriers, such as centralizing course resources, ensuring clear navigation, and providing content in multiple formats.

LO5. Utilize basic learning analytics to measure the effectiveness of inclusive design choices and identify areas for improvement, fostering a cycle of continuous improvement for their courses.
