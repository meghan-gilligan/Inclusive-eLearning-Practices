Course Information & Navigation

Inclusive eLearning Practices is a practical guide to accessible digital course design. This concise, four-hour course is built entirely within Canvas, modeling the very principles of simplicity and organization it teaches. We created this course for busy educators and education students to show you how intentional design can reduce the cognitive load that often causes stress and disengagement in online learning environments. By focusing on Universal Design for Learning (UDL), we will demonstrate how to structure a centralized learning system that works better for all students, particularly those who are neurodivergent and struggle with fragmented, overly complex platforms. You will complete five short, focused modules designed to give you immediate, actionable strategies for building equity and engagement into your own practice.

As educators, we know the goal is to get learners to focus on the content, not the complexity of the platform. However, many students, particularly neurodivergent learners, struggle when navigating multiple digital tools, inconsistent layouts, and complex instructions. This causes what research calls cognitive overload, making it difficult to maintain attention, manage working memory, and process information.

We designed this course to be a proof-of-concept. You will learn about key concepts like Universal Design for Learning (UDL) and accessibility standards, while experiencing these principles in action. By keeping all resources and activities within this single, consistent LMS (Canvas), we demonstrate how clarity, predictability, and centralized design can create a more equitable and engaging experience.

How to Navigate This Course 

This brief guide will help you navigate and feel confident about getting started. The course has been designed with simplicity and consistency in mind. You only need to use a few key links from the Course Navigation Menu on the left side of your screen:

  • Home: The main course page, which includes our Land Acknowledgement, Course Description, and Course Learning Objectives. 

  • Modules: This section guides you through a clear, step-by-step path across all four modules. Navigate through the modules by using the "Next " button located in the bottom right corner of the screen.

  • Discussions: This link takes you directly to all course discussions, where you can share your ideas, respond to classmates, and reflect on what you’ve learned.
