Module 1: Introduction

Why Inclusive eLearning Matters 

Now that you've introduced yourself and begun building our community of practice, let's delve into why this work is important. In Module 1, you'll discover the real barriers neurodivergent students face in poorly designed digital environments, and why solving these barriers benefits all learners.

The Bridge Between Educator Intent and Student Impact

Think of it this way:

Educator Intent assumes: "More tools mean a richer experience, and students can just 'figure out' the technology."

Student Impact reveals: "Every new platform, password, and interface is a new barrier. This 'digital chaos' actively increases cognitive load, creates anxiety, and harms engagement and retention."

You need to see this gap. Before we can learn how to fix the problem (in Module 2), we first need to understand why it is a problem at all. We have to stop seeing our courses as just "content" and start seeing them through the eyes of our students.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this module, learners will be able to:

LO1. Identify at least 3 barriers created by fragmented learning systems;

LO2. Explain how cognitive load theory relates to digital learning accessibility;

LO3. Recognize diverse learning needs across student populations;

LO4. Explain why fragmented learning systems create barriers for neurodivergent students

Your Challenge for This Module

"Accessibility" and "cognitive load" are not just abstract theories; they are deeply personal, lived experiences for your students. In this module, you will focus on building a new, empathetic perspective. We are not starting with solutions yet; we are starting with understanding. You will focus on:

  • Cognitive Barriers: How fragmentation overwhelms executive function.
  • Accessibility Barriers: How multiple platforms fail screen readers and keyboard users.
  • Equity Barriers: How this system disproportionately harms first-generation learners or those with limited tech access.
  • The Lived Experience: What this feels like from a student's perspective.

By the end of this module, you will not just "know" the problem. You will feel the "why" behind this work. You will be able to look at a fragmented course, understand its human impact, and be motivated to find a better way.
