Read: Research Article

Neurodiversity and cognitive load in online learning: A focus group study

Plan for 60 minutes to read and engage meaningfully with the full article. Alternatively, approx. 30 minutes if focusing only on the abstract, introduction, discussion, and conclusion sections.

Summary: Le Cunff et al. (2024) explore how neurotypical and neurodivergent university students experience cognitive load in online learning environments. Through six focus groups with 26 participants, the study identified shared challenges, such as navigating online platforms, maintaining focus, and managing assessments, as well as unique barriers for neurodivergent students, including inaccessible content, poor-quality captions, and unclear curricula. The findings highlight that while all students face cognitive demands online, neurodivergent learners often experience these at greater intensity. The authors recommend inclusive instructional design strategies, such as clearer organization, varied content formats, and accessible materials, to support all learners more effectively.
