Module 2: Introduction

Universal Design for Learning in Action

In Module 1, you discovered how platform fragmentation creates cognitive chaos for neurodivergent students, forcing them to navigate multiple systems, remember different passwords, and toggle between interfaces just to complete basic coursework (Le Cunff et al., 2024). But here's the good news: there's a proven framework that solves this problem and transforms how we design learning experiences for ALL students.

This is where Universal Design for Learning (UDL) comes in. UDL is your pedagogical compass. It’s a framework born from neuroscience that recognizes one fundamental truth: there is no "average learner." UDL instructs you to build in flexibility and choice from the beginning so you remove barriers before students encounter them. You don't need to react to problems; you design them out of existence.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, learners will be able to:

LO1. Define the three principles of Universal Design for Learning (UDL)

LO2. Distinguish between accessible and inaccessible course design using UDL criteria

LO3. Analyze real course examples to identify barriers and UDL solutions

LO4. Apply UDL principles to redesign learning materials for accessibility

Your Challenge for This Module

As you move through this module, you'll develop a new superpower: the ability to look at any course page and immediately spot what's working and what's creating unnecessary barriers. You'll learn to ask: "Does this design open doors or create obstacles?"

By the end of this module, you will understand UDL, and you'll be able to identify specific, concrete examples of inclusive design (and its opposite) in real courses. You'll see through the lens of your most diverse learners, and that lens will transform how you teach.
