Course Examples Before and After UDL

Universal Design for Learning in Action

Below, you'll see three real course scenarios transformed by UDL principles. Each example shows a "before" version that creates barriers and an "after" version that removes them. 

As you explore each comparison, pay close attention to:

  • What specific barriers exist in the "before" version
  • How UDL principles eliminate those barriers in the "after" version
  • Which students would struggle with the "before" design
  • How the "after" design benefits all learners, not just some

Take your time with these examples. They represent real changes you can make in your own courses.
