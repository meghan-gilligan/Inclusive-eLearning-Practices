The goal of this activity is to apply predictive analytics data to inform a proactive, UDL-aligned intervention, demonstrating that the instructor's role is to provide executive function scaffolding and reduce barriers, rather than penalize disengagement.

This discussion is the final activity for Module 4. Apply your knowledge of analytics and UDL principles to recommend a proactive support plan.

 

📝 Scenario 

Scenario: Analytics flags a student who hasn't logged in for 10 days and has missed one minor submission. The predictive model labels them "Medium Risk." The student has self-identified as neurodivergent.

 

🎯 Objective

Demonstrate the ability to interpret predictive analytics as an accessibility flag and propose specific, UDL-aligned interventions that address executive function challenges and social barriers, rather than penalizing a student.

 

❓ Your Task

In your response, please address the following points clearly and concisely:

1. Identify the Barrier:

Based on the data (10-day login gap, missed minor submission), what two core neurocognitive challenges (e.g., related to attention, organization, or social barriers) are most likely being flagged by the system?

Hint: Review the concepts of Cognitive Load and Executive Function from the module reading.

2. Propose a Plan:

Detail three specific, UDL-aligned support strategies you would immediately implement. For each strategy, briefly explain the rationale by referencing the UDL principle or the neurocognitive challenge it addresses.

Hint: Your plan should focus on proactive strategies that reduce social pressure and provide external scaffolding. Avoid using punitive language (e.g., "warning," "failing grades").
