From Understanding to Application 

Excellent work! You've demonstrated your understanding of the three UDL principles and can recognize them in course design examples.

Now comes the exciting part: applying UDL to your own teaching. In this activity, you'll analyze a real lesson from your practice (or one you've experienced as a student) and use AI tools to redesign it with accessibility in mind.

This isn't about perfection, it's about practice. You'll discover how AI can be a powerful partner in inclusive design, while also recognizing where human judgment is still essential.

Your Task:

Step 1: Choose & Analyze Select one lesson/module from a course you teach (or have taken). Briefly analyze it using the UDL framework:

  • Representation: How is content presented? What formats are available or missing?
  • Action & Expression: How do students demonstrate learning? Is there choice?
  • Engagement: What motivates students? Is there flexibility?

Step 2: AI-Assisted Redesign. Visit Eduaide.ai (a free account is available) and utilize it to enhance the accessibility of your lessons. You might:

  • Use the "Scaffolding" tool to create multiple entry points for diverse learners
  • Generate differentiated activities using the "Independent Practice" tools
  • Create engagement activities or alternative assessments
  • Design graphic organizers or varied content formats

Step 3: Reflect & Share Post your experience addressing:

  1. What specific UDL principle(s) did you target?
  2. Which Eduaide.ai tool(s) did you use, and what did it generate?
  3. How did the AI output enhance accessibility? What did you need to modify?
  4. What surprised you about using AI for UDL design?

Post Length: 200-300 words
Include: A brief description of your original lesson and the AI-generated improvement
