WCAG in Practice: Inclusive Design Accessibility Checklist

Your WCAG Toolkit: The Checklist

You don't need to memorize every WCAG guideline. That's what checklists are for.

Download the WCAG Accessibility Checklist [Below]

This checklist breaks WCAG into small, actionable items like:
☐ All images have descriptive alt text
☐ Videos include accurate captions
☐ Text contrast ratio meets 4.5:1 minimum
☐ Headings are used in proper order (H1 → H2 → H3)

Use it as you design or review your course pages. Check off items as you go. You don't need perfection—you need progress.

The Connection to Module 2

Remember the three UDL principles from Module 2? Here's how they map to WCAG:

UDL Principle → WCAG Principle

  • Multiple Means of Representation → Perceivable
  • Multiple Means of Action & Expression → Operable
  • Multiple Means of Engagement → Understandable

When you apply WCAG standards, you're making UDL principles technically accessible. You're turning good intentions into functional reality.
