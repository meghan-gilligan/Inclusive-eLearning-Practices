Evaluate a Course Page

Instructions:

You've discussed design choices with your peers. Now it's time to apply your accessibility lens to a real course page, one you actually use or have created.

Choose a page or module in your choice of LMS that you’ve either created or interacted with.

Identify at least five accessibility issues, such as:

  • Missing alt text for images
  • Unclear or inconsistent headings
  • Low colour contrast
  • Vague or non-descriptive link text
  • Forms or interactive elements that aren’t keyboard accessible

Identify at least five accessibility strengths, such as:

  • Clear, descriptive headings and subheadings
  • Meaningful alt text on images
  • High contrast and readable fonts
  • Descriptive, actionable link text
  • Accessible forms or interactive elements that work with a keyboard

Use the WCAG checklist WCAG2Checklist-2.pdf to document both the issues and the strengths, and recommend any improvements for the issues you found.

Reflect on your findings:

Which changes will have the biggest impact on learner experience? What best practices could you apply to other course pages?

Purpose of this activity:

This activity encourages you to take ownership of accessibility in your LMS. By identifying both strengths and areas for improvement, you reinforce your understanding of WCAG and accessibility principles while gaining confidence in creating inclusive, learner-friendly course content.
