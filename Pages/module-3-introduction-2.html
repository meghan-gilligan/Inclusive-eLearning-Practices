Module 3: Introduction

From UDL Principles to Practical Implementation

In Module 2, you learned the three UDL principles that transform course design: Multiple Means of Representation, Action & Expression, and Engagement. You saw real before-and-after examples and even redesigned a lesson using AI tools. You now understand what makes design inclusive and why it matters.

But here's the question you're probably asking: "Okay, I get the principles...but how do I actually make my Canvas pages, PDFs, and videos technically accessible? What are the actual standards I need to meet?"

This is where WCAG comes in.

The Bridge Between UDL and WCAG

Think of it this way:

  • UDL answers: "What learning barriers exist, and how do I design around them?"
  •  WCAG answers: "What technical standards ensure my content is actually accessible?"

UDL is your pedagogical compass. WCAG is your technical checklist.

They work together. UDL instructs you to provide content in multiple formats (such as video, transcript, and infographic). WCAG tells you exactly how to make that video accessible (with captions that meet specific technical standards) and that transcript readable (with proper heading structure and colour contrast).

You need both. And here's the good news: you don't need to be a technical expert to get started!

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this module, you will be able to:

LO1. Describe WCAG principles (Perceivable, Operable, Understandable, Robust) and their role in reducing barriers for diverse learners

LO2. Connect WCAG technical standards to UDL pedagogical principles

LO3. Identify accessibility issues in course pages using WCAG criteria

LO4. Recommend practical improvements that enhance accessibility for all students

LO5. Evaluate the relationship between aesthetics, usability, and accessibility in course design

Your Challenge for This Module

Accessibility can feel overwhelming when you review the full WCAG documentation (hundreds of success criteria). But you don't need to master everything at once. 

In this module, you'll focus on the high-impact, practical changes that make the biggest difference for neurodivergent and disabled learners:

  • Text alternatives (alt text for images)
  • Headings & structure (proper H1, H2, H3 hierarchy)
  • Colour contrast (readable text on all backgrounds)
  • Captions & transcripts (for video and audio)
  • Keyboard accessibility (no mouse required)

These five areas alone will dramatically improve your course accessibility. Master these first, and you'll have built a strong foundation for everything else.

By the end of this module, you won't just know what WCAG is, you'll be able to look at any course page and immediately spot what's working and what needs fixing. That's your new superpower in action.
