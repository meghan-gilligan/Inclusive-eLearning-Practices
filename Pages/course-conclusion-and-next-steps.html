Your Learning Journey: The Full Arc

Module 1: Understanding the Problem
You discovered how platform fragmentation creates cognitive chaos for neurodivergent students. You learned about cognitive load theory, invisible access work, and the real barriers students face when courses aren't designed with accessibility in mind.

Module 2: The Solution Framework
You learned about Universal Design for Learning (UDL), which encompasses three principles (Representation, Action & Expression, Engagement) that proactively remove barriers. You saw before-and-after examples and redesigned a lesson using AI tools.

Module 3: Technical Standards
You have mastered the WCAG and POUR frameworks (Perceivable, Operable, Understandable, Robust). You learned to identify accessibility barriers and strengths in real course pages using technical standards.

Module 4: Data-Informed Practice
You learned how to use analytics as an accessibility tool, identifying struggling students early through engagement, performance, and predictive data, and intervening with evidence-based support strategies.

You now have:

  • The theoretical understanding (cognitive load, UDL principles)
  • The technical knowledge (WCAG/POUR standards)
  • The practical tools (checklists, intervention strategies)
  • The measurement approach (analytics for early intervention)

Most importantly: You've developed the mindset shift from compliance to inclusive design. You see accessibility not as an afterthought or checkbox, but as the foundation of good teaching!

What You Can Do Right Now

Ready to apply what you've learned? Start here:

Quick Wins (Do These First):

  1. Consolidate: Audit your course and eliminate unnecessary external platforms
  2. Structure: Use proper heading hierarchy (H1, H2, H3) on all pages
  3. Alternatives: Add alt text to images and captions to videos
  4. Predict: Set up Canvas analytics notifications for at-risk students
  5. Intervene: Reach out to students showing irregular engagement patterns

Long-Term Goals:

  • Redesign one full course using UDL principles
  • Create accessibility templates for your department
  • Advocate for institution-wide platform consolidation
  • Share what you've learned with colleagues
  • Continue learning (see resources below)
